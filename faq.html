Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our water drilling services, processes, and policies. If you don't find what you're looking for, please don't hesitate to contact us directly.

What areas do you serve?

We provide water drilling services throughout Kenya, with extensive experience in Elgeyo Marakwet, Uasin Gishu, Nakuru, and West Pokot counties. We also operate in selected regions of South Sudan and Uganda. Our team can advise on the specific availability for your location.

What is your pricing model?

Our pricing is project-specific and depends on several factors including depth requirements, geological conditions, equipment needed, and location accessibility. We provide transparent, customized quotes after conducting a site assessment. Typical borehole projects range from KES 500,000 to KES 2,000,000, including drilling, casing, and basic pump installation.

How long does it take to drill a borehole?

The duration varies based on depth and ground conditions. Most residential boreholes (100-150m depth) take 2-4 days for drilling and casing. Deeper or more complex projects may take up to 7 days. This doesn't include the initial survey (1-2 days) or pump installation (1 day). We provide estimated timelines during the consultation phase.

Do you offer maintenance services?

Yes, we provide comprehensive maintenance packages that include:

  • • Annual system inspections
  • • Pump servicing and repairs
  • • Water quality testing
  • • Yield testing and optimization
  • • Emergency repair services

We recommend at least annual maintenance to ensure optimal performance and longevity of your water system.

What type of equipment do you use?

We utilize modern, well-maintained equipment including:

  • • Rotary drilling rigs (capacity up to 300m depth)
  • • Air percussion (down-the-hole) drills for hard rock
  • • Geophysical survey equipment for water detection
  • • Stainless steel and PVC casing options
  • • Solar and electric pump systems

All equipment meets industry safety and environmental standards.

How do I know if my property has groundwater?

Our hydrogeological survey (conducted before drilling) assesses groundwater potential through:

  • • Geological mapping and analysis
  • • Geophysical surveys (electrical resistivity)
  • • Local hydrological data review
  • • Neighboring borehole performance

While we can't guarantee water until drilling, our surveys have a 90%+ success rate in identifying viable water sources.

What permits are required for drilling?

Requirements vary by location but typically include:

  • • Water Resources Authority (WRA) permit
  • • NEMA environmental impact assessment (for large projects)
  • • County government clearance

We handle all permit applications on your behalf as part of our service, typically adding 2-4 weeks to project timelines.

What warranty do you provide?

We offer:

  • • 1-year warranty on drilling workmanship
  • • Manufacturer warranties on equipment (typically 2-5 years)
  • • 30-day guarantee on pump installation

Warranty terms are detailed in your contract and may vary based on project specifics.
